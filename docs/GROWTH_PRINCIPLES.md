# Growth Psychology Principles — Implementation Guide

This file maps cognitive principles to specific implementations on the MyEdSpace homepage. Every one of these should be visible in your finished page — not just listed, but *implemented* in a way a CRO auditor would recognise.

Where this conflicts with anything in `BRIEF.md`, this file wins.

---

## 1. Hick's Law — fewer choices, faster decisions

The hero asks for ONE thing: pick a grade. That's the only interaction. The full form (name, email) appears later, after that micro-commitment, and is pre-filled with the selected grade.

The phone number does NOT belong on the lead capture form. It's collected post-checkout. Asking for a phone before a parent has committed kills conversion on cold traffic.

## 2. Social proof — show what others have done

Each review card shows: star rating, first name, city + state, and date. The Trustpilot score sits visible near the CTA: "4.8 · Excellent · 1,700+ reviews". A live-counter style line near the form: "Join 21,000+ students already learning."

Anonymous review blocks signal "fake reviews" to a savvy parent. Specificity converts.

## 3. Loss aversion — frame around what they lose

A line near the form: "Every month without structured math support is a month further behind."

The guarantee copy: "If your child doesn't improve, you pay nothing — full refund within 30 days, no questions asked."

Microcopy under CTA: "Cancel before day 8 and you won't be charged."

## 4. Peak-end rule — the last thing they read shouldn't be a stat

The PISA stat moves UP the page (above reviews), not down. The page ends with a warm final CTA section:

- Headline: Your child's math story starts with one class
- Sub: 7 days. Full access. No commitment.
- Button: Start My Child's $7 Trial
- Below: Join 21,000+ students · 30-day money-back · Cancel anytime

The peak moment in the page should be Eddie's credentials section — the SAT 800/800 number deserves a moment of visual weight.

## 5. Commitment & consistency — small yes, then bigger yes

When a grade pill is selected:

- The H1 updates: "Your child's [Algebra I] grade is about to change"
- The page scrolls (or the form animates in) to the lead form
- The CTA button text updates: "Start [Algebra I] Trial for $7"
- The form's grade field is pre-filled and shown as locked-in (with a "change" link)

The parent should feel like every action after clicking the pill is *finishing something they started*, not starting something new.

## 6. Authority — credentials should look like credentials

"Perfect SAT Score 800/800" gets a badge treatment, not body text. A pill near Eddie's photo: "Only the top 1% of teachers pass our screening." Award badges visible. The PISA citation rendered as a journalistic pull-quote, not paragraph text.

## 7. Anchoring — the high number always comes first

Every pricing comparison shows $640+/month FIRST, then $149/month. Always. The hero has a one-line anchor:

> Private tutors charge $640+/month. MyEdSpace is $149.

The trial is always phrased as "Start for just $7" — the word "just" amplifies the contrast.

## 8. Endowment effect — make them feel they already own a spot

After grade selection, show a personalised card:

> ✓ Your spot is reserved
> [Grade] · with Eddie Kang
> Mondays + Thursdays · 4:00 PM PT
> Lock in your $7 trial below

Don't fabricate scarcity. If classes really are filling, say so. If not, lean on "reserved" instead of "only 3 spots left".

## 9. Cognitive load — reduce thinking at every step

Maximum two visible form fields at any moment. Body paragraphs max two lines on desktop, one on mobile. One CTA per section. Use icons alongside feature bullets to reduce reading load:

- 📹 Live classes
- 🤖 24/7 AI coach
- 🎬 Recordings
- 📚 Workbooks

## 10. Storytelling / narrative arc

The page should feel like one story, not a feature list. Order:

1. **The problem** — your child's grade is slipping (hero)
2. **The failed alternatives** — school is too big, tutors are too expensive (Venn comparison)
3. **The solution** — Eddie + the platform
4. **The proof** — reviews + PISA + credentials
5. **The low-risk entry** — $7 trial, 30-day refund
6. **The call to act** — final warm CTA

Add a "before and after" section between the form and Eddie:

> **Before:** Most kids come to us after another bad test. After homework turned into a fight. After a tutor who just did the homework with them.
>
> **After:** Within a few weeks, something changes. They start getting it.

## Acceptance criteria

Your finished page should pass this audit:

- [ ] Hero asks for one thing only (grade pill). No name/email visible above the fold.
- [ ] Selecting a grade rewrites the H1 with that grade
- [ ] Form pre-fills with the selected grade and shows an endowment-style preview card
- [ ] Phone number is NOT on the lead form
- [ ] $640 always appears before $149 in any comparison
- [ ] PISA stat appears above the reviews, not at the page bottom
- [ ] The page ends with a warm final CTA, not a data point
- [ ] Every review has a name, city, state, and date
- [ ] Trustpilot score is visible near every CTA
- [ ] At least one explicit loss-aversion line near a form
- [ ] Eddie's "800/800 SAT" is a visual moment, not a sentence
- [ ] FAQ answers are complete enough that a parent doesn't need to call anyone
